Seamless Computer and Mind Connection

Advancing Brain-Computer Interfaces Through Fiber Photometry

Research by Zainab Afridi

Abstract

This proposal addresses the development of Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCIs) to improve the quality of life for individuals with severe neurological impairments. The primary problem is the current limitations in BCI technology, including issues with signal accuracy, durability, and adaptability to the brain's dynamic environment. The proposed solution focuses on enhancing BCI systems through advanced biosensors, specifically fiber photometry, which offers high sensitivity and minimal invasiveness.

Several candidates for biosensor solutions were evaluated, including two-photon microscopy, head-mounted microscopes, and fiber photometry. Criteria for selection included invasiveness, data accuracy, cost, and mobility. Fiber photometry emerged as the optimal choice due to its superior sensitivity, lower invasiveness, and cost-effectiveness.

Implementation involves addressing challenges such as improving signal-to-noise ratio, enhancing optical fiber design, precise genetic targeting, efficient data analysis, and navigating regulatory and ethical considerations. By overcoming these challenges, the proposed fiber photometry-based BCI system aims to provide reliable and high-quality neural data, facilitating better control of external devices.

The successful implementation of this technology has the potential to significantly enhance the independence and quality of life for patients, making BCIs a transformative tool in neurorehabilitation and beyond.
